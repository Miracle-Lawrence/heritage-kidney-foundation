About Us

FEAT Ndugwas Heritage Kidney Foundation (FNHKF) is a humanitarian, non-profit organization dedicated to supporting indigent and less-privileged individuals suffering from kidney-related diseases. The Foundation exists to reduce the overwhelming financial burden associated with dialysis and kidney transplantation by providing subsidies or full financial support for eligible patients.

Through compassion-driven interventions, advocacy, and partnerships, the Foundation seeks to preserve life, restore hope, and improve the quality of life for those who would otherwise be left without access to critical kidney care.

Our Story

FEAT Ndugwas Heritage Kidney Foundation was founded in 2009, built on a deep passion for humanity and an unwavering commitment to caring for the needy.

What began as a response to the growing number of kidney patients unable to afford dialysis or transplantation has grown into a structured foundation focused on saving lives through financial support and compassionate intervention.

Since its inception, the Foundation has remained steadfast in its mission, placing people at the heart of its work and ensuring that financial hardship does not become a death sentence for those battling kidney disease.

Vision Statement

To create a society where no individual loses their life to kidney disease due to financial constraints.

Mission Statement

To provide structured financial support and compassionate intervention for carefully assessed indigent and less-privileged kidney patients, ensuring that vulnerable individuals have access to life-saving renal treatment.

OUR OBJECTIVES

Objectives of the Foundation

We are committed to improving the lives of kidney patients through compassion, support, and sustainable healthcare solutions.

To provide financial assistance for dialysis treatment for indigent kidney patients.

To fully fund or subsidize kidney transplant procedures for qualified beneficiaries.

To support patients with essential medications related to kidney care.

To promote awareness and early screening for kidney disease.

To collaborate with hospitals, government agencies, and NGOs to improve renal healthcare access.

To advocate for subsidized kidney care policies and improved public health funding.

To reduce kidney-related mortality among vulnerable populations.

Together, we can build a future where every kidney patient has access to care, hope, and a second chance at life.

Our Strategic Partners

We collaborate with institutions and organizations committed to saving lives and expanding access to kidney care across Nigeria.

North East Development Commission (NEDC)

The NEDC plays a vital role in supporting indigent kidney patients from the North-East region. Through this partnership, eligible patients receive full sponsorship for kidney transplant procedures, while others benefit from fully covered dialysis treatment for up to two years. This intervention has significantly reduced the burden on vulnerable patients and given many a second chance at life.

Niger Delta Development Support

Our collaboration with organizations from the Niger Delta region ensures that patients from these communities are not left behind. Through their support, many individuals battling kidney disease are able to access life-saving treatment, easing financial burdens and restoring hope to families.
